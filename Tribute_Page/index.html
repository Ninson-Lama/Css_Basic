Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug examining wheat in a field, 2004. His work developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties sparked the Green Revolution.

The Life and Legacy of Dr. Borlaug

1914
Born in Cresco, Iowa, to a family of Norwegian immigrants.
1937
Earned a Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota.
1944
Began working in Mexico, where he developed semi-dwarf, high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties.
1960s
His wheat varieties helped transform food production in Mexico, Pakistan, and India, sparking the "Green Revolution."
1970
Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to world peace through increasing food supply.
1986
Founded the World Food Prize to honor individuals who improve the quality, quantity, or availability of food worldwide.
2009
Passed away at age 95, leaving behind a legacy of fighting world hunger.
